# Year-Over-Year Financial Performance Analysis

## Project Overview

A price, volume, and mix (PVM) analysis built using sample data from a publicly traded automobile manufacturing company. The goal was to break down year-over-year financial performance across multiple business dimensions, identify the root causes behind revenue and margin movements, and present findings in a clear executive summary.

## Objective

Analyze a multi-dimensional sales dataset to uncover meaningful year-over-year performance trends, diagnose whether changes were driven by volume, pricing, cost efficiency, or product mix shifts, and communicate findings in a format suitable for both finance and non-finance stakeholders.

## Dataset

The dataset contains transactional sales records with the following fields:

- Sales Region (6 regions including U.S. markets, Mexico, and Puerto Rico)
- Product Category (Automobiles, Add Ons, Parts)
- Product Type (Sedan, Truck, Small SUV, Large SUV, Sports Car)
- Product Tier (Luxury, High-End, Standard, Economy, Non-Tier)
- Prior Year and Current Year figures for Quantity Sold, Sales Dollars, Margin Dollars, and Cost Dollars

## Approach

Built pivot tables across four dimensions to understand performance from different angles. Calculated year-over-year changes including revenue growth, volume trends, average selling price, cost per unit, and margin movement to diagnose root causes behind headline numbers.

### Key Findings

- Total revenue grew 1.5% to $320.4M with improving profitability
- Automobiles drove $4.9M in growth while production costs decreased despite higher volume
- Parts revenue declined $4.8M because price per unit dropped significantly, even though more units were sold
- Regional growth was heavily concentrated in North U.S., which contributed $8.6M, creating dependency risk
- Demand for premium Luxury products declined 12%, impacting the most profitable product tier
