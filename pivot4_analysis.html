Key Insights from Pivot #4

Star Performer
High-End: +$6.0M sales (+8.7%), +$3.7M margin — largest growth driver
Volume grew +8.1%, sales grew +8.7% (sales outpacing volume means ASP improved +0.5%). Cost per unit held perfectly flat (-$0.44, essentially zero change), so the growth came with strong efficiency. Margin rate held steady at 57.9%. Share of total sales jumped from 21.9% → 23.5% (+1.6pp). This is the single biggest contributor to the company's sales growth — High-End alone generated more than the total company growth of $4.6M.
Best Margin Story
Standard: +$4.3M margin on +$3.9M sales — margin grew faster than revenue
Volume and sales both grew +4.4% with pricing perfectly stable (ASP moved just $0.21). But the real story is cost: total costs decreased -0.7% despite 5,072 more units. Cost per unit dropped -$20.15 (-4.9%). This drove the biggest margin rate improvement of any tier: 46.2% → 48.8% (+2.6pp). Standard is 29.0% of total sales, so this efficiency gain has meaningful company-wide impact.
Biggest Concern
Luxury: -$4.8M sales (-12.0%), -$2.6M margin — highest-margin tier is shrinking
Volume dropped -10.4% (-2,440 units) and sales fell -$4.8M. This is the highest-margin tier at 64.1%, so every lost Luxury sale hurts disproportionately. ASP declined -1.7% ($1,730 → $1,701), suggesting some price pressure on top of the volume loss. The saving grace: costs were cut aggressively (-15.1%), faster than sales declined (-12.0%), so margin rate actually improved +1.3pp. But in absolute dollars, $2.6M in margin was lost. Share shrank from 12.8% → 11.1%.
Quiet Erosion
Non-Tier: Largest tier (35% of sales) seeing margin deterioration
Volume grew +2.8% but sales dipped -0.3%, meaning ASP fell -3.0% ($65.09 → $63.14). More concerning: costs grew +1.7% while sales declined -0.3%. This is the worst cost-vs-sales pattern of any tier. Margin rate dropped from 48.3% → 47.3% (-1.1pp) — the only tier with margin rate decline. Lost $1.4M in margin dollars. As the largest single tier at 34.9% of sales, this erosion drags on the entire company.
Mix Shift
Favorable shift toward High-End and Standard, away from Luxury and Non-Tier
Growing share: High-End (21.9% → 23.5%, +1.6pp) and Standard (28.2% → 29.0%, +0.8pp) — both have strong and improving margins.

Shrinking share: Luxury (12.8% → 11.1%, -1.7pp) and Non-Tier (35.6% → 34.9%, -0.6pp).

The net effect is mixed: losing Luxury share hurts (it's 64% margin), but gaining High-End share helps (58% margin), and Standard's +2.6pp margin improvement makes its growing share very positive. Non-Tier's shrinking share is actually helpful since its margin is deteriorating.
Minor Item
Economy: Small and stable — not material
Only 1.5% of total sales ($4.7M). Volume dipped -4.5%, sales declined -2.7%, but ASP actually rose +1.8% ($608 → $619). Margin rate improved +0.8pp. Costs declined -4.3%, faster than sales. Overall a healthy small segment, but too small to move the needle. Not worth mentioning in the exec email unless asked.
